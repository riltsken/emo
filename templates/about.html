Developers

Samuel Toriel is a student at UCF in Orlando, FL and programming enthusiast who enjoys learning about people and finding new web applications to poke fun with online.

Alexander Meng is another UCF student. He's a computer science major and a (clearly) amateur graphic designer.

How did this come about?

Alex and Sam are co-workers who were looking for a project to work on together in their off time. Sam was recently thinking about getting a journal, or something to keep track of things happening in his life and how he felt at the time. The current mood tracking programs out there, while good, were too focused or too simple. When Sam bought up the idea of an online mood tracking application, Alex readily agreed with the idea. He wanted to take it one step further and add statistics for people to more easily see changes in their mood.

How did you make it?

Emovolution was made using the Python programming language and the Django framework. For the front end and statistics, we used jQuery, jqPlot, and jQuery Sparklines. We also used numerous open-source django applications, you guys rock!
